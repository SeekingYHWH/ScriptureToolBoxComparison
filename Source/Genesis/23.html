Chapter 23

Sarah's death and burial -- Purchase of the field of Ephron.

1)And Sarah was an hundred and twenty-seven and twenty years old:, theseand wereshe died; and thus ended the years of the life of Sarah.

2)And Sarah died in Kirjatharba; the same is now called Hebron, in the land of Canaan: and.

3)And Abraham came to mourn for Sarah, and to weep for her, his wife which was dead.

34)And Abraham stood up from before his dead, and spake unto the sons of Heth, saying, 4) I am a stranger and a sojourner with you:; give me a possession of a buryingplaceburying-place with you, that I may bury my dead out of my sight.

5)And the children of Heth answered Abraham, saying unto him, 6) Hear us, my lord:; thou art a mighty prince among us:; in the choicechoicest of our sepulchressepulchers bury thou thy dead; none of us shall withhold from thee his sepulchresepulcher, but that thou mayest bury thy dead.

76)And Abraham stood up, and bowed himself to the people of the land, evenand to the children of Heth. 8)And; and he communed with them, saying,

7)If it be your mind that I should bury my dead out of my sight;, hear me, and entreat for me to Ephron the son of Zohar for me, 9)That that he may give me the cave of Machpelah, which he hath, which is in the end of his field; for

8)For, as much money as it is worth he shall have, if he will give it me for a possession of a buryingplaceburying-place amongstamong you.

13)If thou wilt take it of me, I will give thee money for the field; take it of me, and I will bury my dead there, but I will give thee money for it.

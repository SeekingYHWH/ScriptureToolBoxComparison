Chapter 5

The unclean spirit at the tombs -- Healing of Jairus' daughter.

1)And they came over unto the other side of the sea, into the country of the Gadarenes.,

2)And when he was come out of the ship, immediately there met him out of the tombs, a man with an unclean spirit, who had been dwelling among the tombs.

3)Who had his dwelling among the tombs; andAnd no man could bind him, no, not with chains: 4)Because; because that he had been often bound with fetters, and chains, and the chains had been plucked asunder by him, and the fetters broken in pieces:; neither could any man tame him.

54)And always, night and day, he was in the mountains, and in the tombs, crying, and cutting himself with stones.

65)But when he saw Jesus afar off, he ran and worshipped him, 7)And and cried with a loud voice, and said, What have I to do with thee, Jesus, thou Son of the most high God? I adjure thee by God, that thou torment me not. 8) For he said unto him, Come out of the man, thou unclean spirit.

96)And he askedcommanded him saying, What isDeclare thy name?. And he answered, saying, My name is Legion:; for we are many.

107)And he besought him much, that he would not send them away out of the country.

118)Now there was there, nigh unto the mountains, a great herd of swine feeding.

129)And all the devils besought him, saying, Send us into the swine, that we may enter into them. 13) And forthwith Jesus gave them leave.

10)And the unclean spirits went out, and entered into the swine:; and the herd ran violently down a steep place into the sea, (they were about two thousand;,) and were choked in the sea.

1411)And they that fed the swine fled, and told itthe people in the city, and in the country, all that was done unto the swine.

12)And they went out to see what it was that was done. 15) And they comecame to Jesus, and seesaw him that was possessed with the devil, and had the legionLegion, sitting, and clothed, and in his right mind:; and they were afraid.

1613)And they that saw itthe miracle, told them that came out, how it befell to him that was possessed with the devil, and alsohow the devil was cast out, and concerning the swine.

1714)And they began immediately to pray him to depart out of their coasts.

1815)And when he was come into the ship, he that had been possessed with the devil, spoke to Jesus, and prayed him that he might be with him.

1916)Howbeit, Jesus suffered him not, but saithsaid unto him, Go home to thy friends, and tell them how great things the Lord hath done for thee, and hath had compassion on thee.

2017)And he departed, and began to publish in Decapolis, how great things Jesus had done for him:; and all menthat heard him did marvel.

2118)And when Jesus washad passed over again by ship unto the other side, much people gathered unto him:; and he was nigh unto the sea.

2219)And, behold, there cometh one of the rulers of the synagogue, Jairus by name; and when he saw him, he fell at his feet, 23)And and besought him greatly, saying, My little daughter lieth at the point of death: I pray thee,; come and lay thy hands on her, that she may be healed; and she shall live.

2420)And Jesushe went with him; and much people followed him, and thronged him.

2521)And a certain woman, which had an issue of blood twelve years, 26)And and had suffered many things of many physicians, and had spent all that she had, and was nothing bettered, but rather grew worse, 27)When; when she had heard of Jesus, she came in the press behind, and touched his garment. 28)For; for she said, If I may touch but his clothes, I shall be whole.

2922)And straightway the fountain of her blood was dried up; and she felt in her body that she was healed of that plague.

3023)And Jesus, immediately knowing in himself that virtue had gone out of him, turned him about in the press, and said, Who touched my clothes?

3124)And his disciples said unto him, Thou seest the multitude thronging thee, and sayest thou, Who touched me?

3225)And he looked round about to see her that had done this thing. 33)But; but the woman, fearing and trembling, knowing what was done in her, came and fell down before him, and told him all the truth.

3426)And he said unto her, Daughter, thy faith hath made thee whole; go in peace, and be whole of thy plague.

3527)While he yet spake, there came from the ruler of the synagogue's house, certaina whichman who said, Thy daughter is dead:; why troublest thou the Master any further?

3628)As soon as he spake, Jesus heard the word that was spoken, heand saithsaid unto the ruler of the synagogue, Be not afraid, only believe.

3729)And he suffered no man to follow him, save Peter, and James, and John the brother of James.

3830)And he cometh to the house of the ruler of the synagogue, and seeth the tumult, and them that wept and wailed greatly.

3931)And when he was come in, he saithsaid unto them, Why make ye this ado, and weep? theThe damsel is not dead, but sleepeth. 40) And they laughed him to scorn.

32)But when he had put them all out, he taketh the father and the mother of the damsel, and them that were with him, and entereth in where the damsel was lying.;

4133)And he took the damsel by the hand, and said unto her, Talitha cumi; which is, being interpreted, Damsel, I say unto thee, ariseArise.

4234)And straightway the damsel arose, and walked; for she was of the age of twelve years old. And they were astonished with a great astonishment.

4335)And he charged them straitly that no man should know it; and commanded that something should be given her to eat.

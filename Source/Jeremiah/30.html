Chapter 30

The return of the Jews -- Wrath shall fall on the wicked.

1)The word that came to Jeremiah from the LORDLord, saying,

2)Thus speaketh the LORDLord God of Israel, saying, Write thee all the words that I have spoken unto thee in a book.

3)For, lo, the days come, saith the LORDLord, that I will bring again the captivity of my people Israel and Judah, saith the LORD:Lord; and I will cause them to return to the land that I gave to their fathers, and they shall possess it.

4)And these are the words that the LORDLord spake concerning Israel and concerning Judah.

5)For thus saith the LORDLord; We have heard a voice of trembling, of fear, and not of peace.

6)Ask ye now, and see whether a man doth travail with child? wherefore do I see every man with his hands on his loins, as a woman in travail, and all faces are turned into paleness?

7)Alas! for that day is great, so that none is like it:; it is even the time of Jacob's trouble,; but he shall be saved out of it.

8)For it shall come to pass in that day, saith the LORDLord of hosts, that I will break his yoke from off thy neck, and will burst thy bonds, and strangers shall no more serve themselves of him:;

9)But they shall serve the LORDLord their God, and David their king, whom I will raise up unto them.

10)Therefore fear thou not, O my servant Jacob, saith the LORDLord; neither be dismayed, O Israel:; for, lo, I will save thee from afar, and thy seed from the land of their captivity; and Jacob shall return, and shall be in rest, and be quiet, and none shall make him afraid.

11)For I am with thee, saith the LORDLord, to save thee:; though I make a full end of all nations whither I have scattered thee, yet will I will not make a full end of thee:; but I will correct thee in measure, and will not leave thee altogether unpunished.

12)For thus saith the LORDLord, Thythy bruise is not incurable, andalthough thy woundwounds isare grievous.

13)ThereIs isthere none to plead thy cause, that thou mayest be bound up:? Hast thou hast no healing medicines.?

14)AllHave all thy lovers have forgotten thee;, do they not seek thee? not; forFor I have wounded thee with the wound of an enemy, with the chastisement of a cruel one, for the multitude of thine iniquityiniquities; because thy sins wereare increased.

15)Why criest thou for thine affliction? Is thy sorrow isincurable? incurableIt was for the multitude of thine iniquity:iniquities, and because thy sins wereare increased, I have done these things unto thee.

16)ThereforeBut all they that devour thee shall be devoured; and all thine adversaries, every one of them, shall go into captivity; and they that spoil thee shall be a spoil, and all that prey upon thee will I give for a prey.

17)For I will restore health unto thee, and I will heal thee of thy wounds, saith the LORDLord; because they called thee an Outcast, saying, This is Zion, whom no man seeketh after.

18)Thus saith the LORDLord; Behold, I will bring again the captivity of Jacob's tents, and have mercy on his dwellingplacesdwelling places; and the city shall be builded upon her own heap, and the palace shall remain after the manner thereof.

19)And out of them shall proceed thanksgiving and the voice of them that make merry:; and I will multiply them, and they shall not be few; I will also glorify them, and they shall not be small.

20)Their children also shall be as aforetime, and their congregation shall be established before me, and I will punish all that oppress them.

21)And their nobles shall be of themselves, and their governor shall proceed from the midst of them; and I will cause him to draw near, and he shall approach unto me:; for who is this that engaged his heart to approach unto me? saith the LORDLord.

22)And ye shall be my people, and I will be your God.

23)Behold, the whirlwind of the LORDLord goeth forth with fury, a continuing whirlwind:; it shall fall with pain upon the head of the wicked.

24)The fierce anger of the LORDLord shall not return, until he hathhave done it, and until he have performed the intents of his heart:; in the latter days ye shall consider it.
